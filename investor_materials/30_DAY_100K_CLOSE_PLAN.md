# StockBaller - 30 Day Plan to Close First GBP 100K

## Objective
Close GBP 100,000 in committed pre-seed funding within 30 days using an angel-first strategy (SEIS-compatible), with clear weekly targets and daily execution.

## Funding Strategy Snapshot
- Round context: GBP 250K pre-seed target.
- Immediate goal: secure first GBP 100K as momentum capital.
- Target check sizes: GBP 10K-50K.
- Ideal mix:
  - 1 lead/anchor angel: GBP 25K-40K
  - 2-4 supporting angels: GBP 10K-25K each
  - 1 strategic sports/crypto backer: GBP 10K-20K

## Raise Math (Practical)
- Assumed conversion funnel:
  - 120 targeted outbound touches
  - 20-25 meaningful replies
  - 12-15 meetings
  - 5-7 serious due diligence conversations
  - 3-5 commitments
- Minimum close scenarios:
  - 2 x GBP 25K + 2 x GBP 12.5K + 1 x GBP 25K = GBP 100K
  - 4 x GBP 20K + 1 x GBP 20K strategic = GBP 100K

## Non-Negotiable Materials (Day 1-3)
Before heavy outreach, lock these assets:
1. Deck: concise 12-15 slides with GBP-based financials only.
2. One-pager: clear problem, solution, traction, ask, use of funds.
3. Data room: single folder with core docs and links.
4. 2-minute product demo: stable, no setup friction.
5. Legal/fundraising docs:
   - SAFE or equivalent subscription docs
   - SEIS status update note
   - cap table snapshot
6. KPI snapshot (1 page):
   - active users
   - transaction volume
   - retention trend
   - waitlist growth
   - current roadmap milestones

## Weekly Plan

### Week 1 (Days 1-7): Build Pipeline + Book Meetings
Target outcome: 8-10 investor meetings booked.

Actions:
1. Build segmented target list (60 names):
   - 25 UK angels (SEIS-friendly)
   - 15 crypto-native investors
   - 10 sports/media strategic angels
   - 10 micro-VC/accelerator contacts
2. Prioritize warm intros first:
   - Ask 10 contacts for intros.
   - Send intro-ready blurb + deck link.
3. Outbound cadence:
   - 12-15 personalized emails/day for 5 days.
   - 10 LinkedIn connection requests/day.
4. Daily follow-up block:
   - Follow up all non-responders on day 5.
5. Meeting script prep:
   - 3-minute narrative
   - 30-second traction proof
   - clear ask: "We are closing first GBP 100K by [date]."

Week 1 KPI:
- 60 total investor targets loaded
- 50+ outbound messages sent
- 8-10 meetings scheduled

### Week 2 (Days 8-14): Run Meetings + Qualify Fast
Target outcome: 4-6 investors moved to diligence.

Actions:
1. Run meetings with strict structure:
   - problem/market (2 min)
   - product/demo (4 min)
   - traction + economics (4 min)
   - roadmap + ask (3 min)
2. Ask every meeting for 2 intros.
3. Same-day follow-up email:
   - deck
   - one-pager
   - specific diligence answers
4. Score each investor after each call:
   - A: likely to commit
   - B: interested but slow
   - C: low probability
5. Concentrate on A and B only.

Week 2 KPI:
- 10-12 meetings completed cumulative
- 4-6 investors in active diligence
- 2+ soft-circle signals (verbal intent)

### Week 3 (Days 15-21): Convert Interest to Commitments
Target outcome: first GBP 40K-60K soft-circled.

Actions:
1. Anchor investor push:
   - focus top 3 highest-conviction investors.
   - ask direct close question: "Are you willing to anchor GBP 25K-40K if we close by [date]?"
2. Start urgency loop:
   - weekly investor update email with traction and progress.
   - include line: "Round is now X% soft-circled."
3. Resolve objections quickly:
   - legal/regulatory note
   - data supplier reliability plan
   - execution roadmap and team coverage
4. Send draft docs early to ready investors.
5. Book second calls for all A-tier investors.

Week 3 KPI:
- GBP 40K-60K soft-circled
- 2-3 investors requesting docs
- 1 likely anchor identified

### Week 4 (Days 22-30): Close and Collect Commitments
Target outcome: GBP 100K committed.

Actions:
1. Set close deadline:
   - "First close date: [specific date]"
2. Create final allocation pressure:
   - communicate remaining allocation clearly.
3. Final call sprint:
   - 6-8 close-focused calls.
4. Paperwork workflow:
   - docs sent within same day
   - signature follow-up every 24 hours until done
5. Keep momentum visible:
   - share key milestone updates (product, partnerships, user growth)
   - keep message factual and brief

Week 4 KPI:
- GBP 100K committed
- 3-5 signed commitments
- clear pathway for remaining round (next GBP 150K)

2. 09:30-11:30: outbound and follow-ups.
3. 11:30-14:30: investor calls.
4. 14:30-16:00: diligence responses and data room updates.
5. 16:00-17:00: intro requests and next-day scheduling.
6. 17:00-17:30: CRM updates and metrics.

## Core Messaging to Improve Conversion
Use this short positioning in calls and emails:
1. "We are not selling betting; we are building a performance-linked sports asset market."
2. "We already have product, bot-based liquidity infrastructure, and oracle-driven pricing flow."
3. "We are raising to hit specific value inflection points: mainnet reliability, first 1,000 users, and B2B pilots from Q1 2027."

## Objection Handling (Fast Responses)

### "Regulatory risk is too high"
Response:
- We are operating with a staged compliance approach.
- Budget and workstream already allocated for legal guidance and UK pathway clarity.
- Product design emphasizes asset ownership mechanics over binary gambling outcomes.

### "How do you acquire users cheaply?"
Response:
- Football-native acquisition channels (creator and community driven).
- Built-in engagement loops: watchlists, alerts, social competition roadmap.
- Referral mechanics tied to weekly leaderboard outcomes.

### "What if market liquidity is weak at launch?"
Response:
- Existing bot fleet supports baseline liquidity and activity.
- Gradual user ramp + roster expansion plan.
- Market operations controls via admin tooling already in place.

## Investor CRM Fields (Track Daily)
- Name
- Segment (Angel/Crypto/Sports/VC)
- Warm/Cold source
- Stage (Contacted/Meeting 1/Meeting 2/Diligence/Docs/Signed)
- Likely check size
- Conviction score (1-5)
- Next action + due date

## Red Flags During Raise
1. Over-explaining technical details before proving market and traction.
2. Taking low-quality meetings that consume founder bandwidth.
3. No explicit close date.
4. Slow follow-up (more than 24 hours).
5. Inconsistent numbers across deck, one-pager, and Q and A responses.

## Success Criteria by Day 30
1. At least GBP 100K committed.
2. At least one anchor investor secured.
3. Minimum 3 signed commitments.
4. Clear shortlist for next GBP 150K close.
5. Updated investor update list for biweekly momentum emails.

## Day 31+ Next Steps
1. Announce first close to pipeline and reopen remaining allocation.
2. Push second close quickly while momentum is high.
3. Publish progress update with milestones achieved from first-close capital.
